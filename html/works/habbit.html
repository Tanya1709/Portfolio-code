Overview

Introduction

Habbit is a platform that connects its users with relevant mentors/experts for them to learn hobbies and skills through habit-based communities , with a focus on experiential education where individuals can learn, interact and create.

Objective

To create mobile application from scratch for learners of habbit- where they discover new hobbies, learn them in a way they are comfortable, share their creation within their community and also keep practicing the skill to actually master it.

Research

We started the project having a vague idea that we hope to help kids and young adults to learn new skills through interactive learning platform. However, based on our primary research and stakeholders interviews we focussed on anyone who wanted to learn a new skill or upgrade in an existing one.

Domain Research

To better understand the problem space, we conducted a literature review and a competitive analysis.For the competitive analysis, we focused on the existing e-learning platforms for skill development.

Products we analyzed for the Competitive Analysis



Our main findings include:

  • Platforms enabling students to meet relevant mentors in online mode is gaining more acceptance
  • Access to free content on the platform to provide an experience is driving adoption to freemium model, resulting in increase in paid subscription on these platforms.
  • Different users preferred different way of learning- some within a group, some individually and some with just their friends
  • Kids report higher satisfaction to video based content. Around 50% of online users indicate preference for video based content on these platform
  • The decision to purchase online solution is influenced by stakeholders such as parents, peers, faculty and content available on internet
  • Peer review and feedback play a key role in discovery and selection of platform which is why these platforms have efficient feedback mechanism

The results motivated us to:

  • To design application for Live interactive class
  • To give trial course for free to give them the first hand experience of the product .
  • Having different services according to the needs of the user
  • Designing a effective and efficient feedback mechanism

Surveys & Interviews


Surveys

We used survey to understand 1) What are the expectations of the people who would use habbit for skill development 2) What were the pain points they encountered with other e-learning platforms they might have used




Interviews

We then conducted semi-structured interviews with some of the existing users of habbit and parents whose kids use habbit to- 1) To understand the pain points of existing users of habbit and if they have any suggestions 2) dive deeper into problems parents face when they enroll/ motivate their kids for any skill development

We created an affinity map as a group to identify themes from our qualitative interview data.

Defining Our Services

Based on the research done, we decide to include three services in our application which would cater to the needs of the user and would give them a personalized experience.

Usability Testing

The aims of our evaluation sessions are 1) to identify usability problems with our current design and 2) to assess the learnability of our application.

We conducted our user testing through maze, which provided us with detailed insights regarding our designs.




Observed issues and suggestions from users

  • Easier access to important information like what the session is about
  • The button Practice didn't quite communicate that users could get the information clicking on that.
  • Few users struggles to understand what the practice session was about
  • Problem in visibility of service tabs


Conclusion

Not everything went as planned but the important thing is we learned. Based on issues identified we made some minor changes in the UI. The project was my first exposure to industry and was like a roller coaster ride. I struggled, hustled and listened because everything made me a better designer but even better team player. Our design was well accepted by the users because of which habbit has raised $320,000 in pre-seed round and has 15k active users with 500 mentors on board, which definitely feels like an achievement.
